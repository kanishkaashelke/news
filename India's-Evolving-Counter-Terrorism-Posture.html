The 2025 Crisis

India's New Counter-Terrorism Doctrine Takes Shape

The Catalyst: A Barbaric Act

The fragile peace in South Asia was shattered on April 22, 2025, by a horrific terror attack in Pahalgam, Indian-administered Kashmir. This event was not just another cross-border incident; its brutal nature and communal targeting directly triggered India's unprecedented response and a fundamental shift in its national security policy.

26
Civilian Deaths in Pahalgam

Pakistan-backed attackers deliberately targeted Hindu tourists, a move designed to incite communal violence and destabilize India from within.

17
Civilian Deaths in Poonch

In retaliation, Pakistan's heavy shelling on Poonch, Jammu, resulted in significant civilian casualties, including a Sikh temple Ragi and children.

Timeline of a Four-Day Conflict

The crisis unfolded rapidly, escalating from a terror attack to a multi-domain military confrontation in a matter of weeks, culminating in a tense, internationally-mediated ceasefire.

1

April 22: Pahalgam Terror Attack

26 civilians are killed, sparking national outrage in India.

2

May 7: Operation Sindoor Begins

India launches air and missile strikes on 9 alleged terror sites in Pakistan and PoK.

3

May 7-9: Escalation & Drone Duels

Pakistan responds with heavy shelling and launches its own operations. Both sides engage in aerial dogfights and drone warfare.

4

May 10: Ceasefire Agreed

Following a call initiated by Pakistan's DGMO and US mediation, a ceasefire is announced.

5

June 9: A New Red Line Drawn

EAM Jaishankar publicly warns that India will not hesitate to strike deep inside Pakistan if provoked again.

Operation Sindoor: India's Kinetic Response

India's military response was designed to be punitive and targeted, striking deep into Pakistan's heartland. The operation aimed to dismantle terror infrastructure across the entire operational chain, from launchpads in PoK to headquarters in Punjab province. India claimed over 100 terrorists were killed in these strikes.

Beyond Bombs: A Multi-Domain Strategy

Operation Sindoor was more than a military operation; it was a comprehensive, state-level coercive strategy. India unleashed a series of non-kinetic measures designed to impose severe and lasting strategic, political, and economic costs on Pakistan.

  • Economic Pressure: Indus Waters Treaty held in abeyance, a move impacting 80% of Pakistan's agricultural land. Key trade routes were severed.
  • Diplomatic Isolation: Visas for all Pakistanis were revoked, and diplomatic staff in New Delhi were significantly reduced.
  • Information Warfare: India launched a proactive campaign to counter Pakistani misinformation with transparent facts and operational details.

The New Doctrine: Deterrence by Punishment

The 2025 crisis formalized a fundamental shift in India's security posture. The previous policy of "strategic restraint" has been replaced by a proactive doctrine of "deterrence by punishment." This new framework treats any future terror attack as an act of war, eliminating the distinction between terrorists and their state sponsors.

TRIGGER

A "barbaric" terror attack emanating from Pakistan.

→

RESPONSE: ACT OF WAR

Immediate, multi-domain retaliation. Deep kinetic strikes on terror infrastructure combined with severe economic and diplomatic punishment.

→

GOAL

Impose unsustainable costs to deter future attacks and dismantle terror networks over time ("Cumulative Deterrence").

The World Responds

The crisis drew immediate global attention. While most nations urged restraint, there was a clear divergence in responses, reflecting complex geopolitical alignments in the region.

Strong Condemnation of Terror & Support for India

Nations that strongly condemned the Pahalgam attack and expressed solidarity with India's fight against cross-border terrorism.
