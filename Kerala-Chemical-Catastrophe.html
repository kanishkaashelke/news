Disaster Timeline

June 7, 2025

Departure

MV Wan Hai 503 departs from Colombo, Sri Lanka, en route to Mumbai, India.

June 9, 09:30 AM

Distress Alert

Indian Navy's IFC-IOR receives a distress alert reporting an internal container explosion and fire onboard the vessel.

June 9, ~12:30 PM

Catastrophe Strikes

A severe internal explosion occurs, leading to a major fire. Within hours, 18 of the 22 crew members abandon the rapidly deteriorating vessel.

June 9, 16:30 PM

Rescue Operation

The Indian Navy ship INS Surat successfully evacuates the 18 crew members from lifeboats, demonstrating a swift and effective response.

June 10, Ongoing

Persistent Emergency

The vessel remains adrift, listing 10-15 degrees and burning. Firefighting efforts continue amidst ongoing explosions, as the search for 4 missing crew members is underway.

The Human Cost

Of the 22 crew members aboard, the swift SAR operation led by the Indian Navy and Coast Guard was critical. However, the incident resulted in injuries and a search for those still missing.

18 Rescued Crew

Evacuated by INS Surat and transported to New Mangalore Port. The successful rescue under volatile conditions highlights the effectiveness of the coordinated response.

5-6 Injured

Two individuals remain in critical condition with severe burns (30-40%) and respiratory injuries. Three others with less severe injuries have been discharged.

Coordinated Emergency Response

The emergency triggered a massive, multi-agency response, demonstrating strong international and domestic cooperation to save lives and contain the disaster.

Grave Ecological Threat

This disaster poses a multi-faceted environmental risk far exceeding a simple oil spill, occurring during a critical fish breeding season and marking the second major hazardous spill in the region in under a month.

Compounded Crisis

The second major hazardous cargo disaster off Kerala in 3 weeks, following the sinking of MSC ELSA 3. This poses a "more serious threat" due to the volume and variety of chemicals.

Debris Drift Alert

INCOIS simulations predict lost containers and pollutants will drift towards the Kerala coast, impacting areas between Kozhikode and Kochi, threatening shorelines and navigation.

Economic Devastation

The repeated incidents have devastated the local fishing economy, with a sharp decrease in demand for fish due to contamination fears, impacting thousands of livelihoods.
